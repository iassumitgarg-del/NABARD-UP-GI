How to Apply for GI Authorized User Registration

A complete step-by-step guide with document templates for NGOs, Self-Help Groups (SHGs), and individual producers.

1

Verify Product & Proprietor

Confirm the official GI Name, Application Number, and Registered Proprietor organization (e.g. Pooja Handloom for Mirzapur Dari) from the official registry list.

2

Gather Proof of Production

Collect Voter ID/Aadhar, Artisan Card, and business or land ownership proof (Patta, land tax receipts) showing your production unit is within the registered GI region.

3

Obtain No Objection Certificate

Get a signed Consent Letter/NOC from the Registered Proprietor of the GI, certifying that your products match the official quality standards.

4

Fill Form GI-3A & Submit

Complete Form GI-3A in triplicate, attach the Statement of Case, NOC, and pay the ₹10 statutory fee. Send it to the GI Registry in Chennai.

What is a GI Tag?

A Geographical Indication (GI) is an intellectual property tag given to products that originate from a specific region, where their unique qualities, reputation, or characteristics are essentially attributable to that geographic location. Examples include Banarasi Silk and Aligarh Locks.

Who is an Authorized User?

Under the Indian GI Act, producers registered in Part B of the GI Register are recognized as Authorized Users. Buyers should confirm current registration status through IP India and assess the product against the applicable GI specification.
